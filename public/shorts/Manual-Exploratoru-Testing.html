The Magic and Mystery of Manual Exploratory Testing

There is no magic nor mystery about Exploratory Testing, it can only logically cover 3 areas:

  • Tests that need more time and thought to conjure up
  • Tests that you couldn't automate
  • Tests that you didn't want to automate
The magic and mystery is not the test, but the tester.

The best Exploratory Testers have a treasure of capabilities:

  • Innate qualities of; attention to detail, tenacity, lateral thought, suspicious minds etc
  • Experts at testing techniques; equivalence partioning, boundary analysis, model based etc
  • Indepth system knowledge of; upstream, downstream, dependent, and consumer apps
  • Experience of gotchas
  • Knowledge of the tech stack and potential weakness; browsers, containers, databases etc
  • Knowledge of the app code and the architecture
  • An empathy for the business imperatives
  • Expertise with tools; postman, firebug, wireshark, debuggers, datadog, soapui etc
  • A 'nose' for identifying areas that might surface issues
  • The diplomacy to escalate issues in a constructive and collaborative manner
